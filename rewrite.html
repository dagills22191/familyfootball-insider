Parity is the polite word. FamilyFootball has spent four years operating more like a regional airport during a thunderstorm: everybody eventually gets a turn near the front, nobody knows where the luggage went, and the loudest person at the gate is usually not helping.

The championship has changed hands every season. That sounds like competitive balance until you inspect the machinery. Three of the four champions won more regular-season games than their weekly scoring would predict. The 2024 champion ranked ninth in points. The 2025 champion ranked eighth. This league does not require a season-long masterpiece. It requires a playoff invitation, two functioning Sundays and the emotional flexibility to call the result “clutch.”

To separate quality from scheduling, each weekly score was compared with all 11 opponents. Beat eight scores in a week, earn 8/11 of an expected win. Add 56 weeks, and the mythology starts losing arguments to arithmetic. Some franchises have been exactly what their records say. Some have received concierge service from the schedule. One has been mugged repeatedly in broad daylight.

Division I

Champions with receipts

They have the rings. The numbers are here only to determine how loudly everyone else should complain.

The benchmark, with favorable lighting

jgirsby

36–20 record1 title2 finals+3.09 schedule wins

The best record in league history belongs here, full stop. jgirsby has two finals, three playoff trips and eight weekly high scores against only one weekly low. That is not a hot streak. That is infrastructure.

Now the subpoena: the 2024 championship team finished ninth in regular-season scoring and produced only 6.55 all-play expected wins before posting a 9–5 record. jgirsby did not flatten the league that year. jgirsby found the postseason side entrance, made direct eye contact with security and walked out carrying the trophy. Great franchises call that situational excellence. Everyone else calls it checking the schedule twice.

No franchise has hit the weekly ceiling more often. Nine times in 56 regular-season weeks, alyssagenther posted the league’s top score. The draft portfolio explains it: Tyreek Hill, Amon-Ra St. Brown and Malik Nabers opened the last three drafts. In full PPR, this is less a preference than a hostile takeover of the reception column.

The season was not dominant. The ending was. Wall Street would call that timing the market; fantasy managers call it skill immediately after it works.

A concrete floor beneath a locked playoff ceiling

sammyyy29

33–23 record3 playoff trips0 finals1 weekly low

Across 56 regular-season weeks, sammyyy29 finished last in scoring once. Once. Some teams have done it in a month. This is the league’s steadiest weekly floor, backed by nine wide receivers in the first five rounds across four drafts and exactly the kind of boring competence that produces annual contention.

It has not produced a final. The 2025 team went 11–3, received 2.91 more wins than its all-play performance predicted and then lost both postseason games. That is not a collapse so much as a full audit arriving at the worst possible time. The regular season wrote a glowing recommendation letter. The playoffs called the references.

The league’s control group

26–30 record26.10 expected wins2 playoff trips1 weekly high

Actual wins: 26. Expected wins: 26.10. Schedule luck: negative one-tenth of a win. If the rest of the league is a laboratory accident, divinethought is the untouched sample labeled “normal.”

Normal does not mean predictable. The finishes are sixth, 12th, fourth and ninth, a sequence that reads less like a trajectory than four numbers selected during a fire drill. The first rounds show conviction—Justin Jefferson twice, Austin Ekeler, Ashton Jeanty—and the early rounds lean heavily toward running back. Yet only one weekly high in four years suggests the roster often arrives near contention without ever kicking down the door. It knocks. It waits. Sometimes the playoffs answer.

The 2026 outlook

The draft order is not set. The pressure is.

Sleeper has not assigned the 2026 draft order yet, so any slot projection remains exactly that: a projection. What the history does establish is more useful. ITouchd0wnThere owns the strongest title-free process. sammyyy29 has the most stable weekly floor. D1LLIGAF has the largest case for positive regression. mmcatee01 has the clearest case for the schedule sending an invoice. And every champion should remember the league’s only functioning tradition: the defending winner has never finished better than fifth.

Four years are not enough to declare permanent owner tendencies, but they are more than enough to create expectations. Dawnieo will make the room watch the quarterback board. alyssagenther will test the receiver supply. jgirsby will wait on quarterback and act as though patience was invented locally. ozarklu will make an excellent first selection and force the analysts to keep working.

The league has no dynasty because it has no stable hierarchy. It has contenders, variance, schedule luck and a two-week playoff that treats four months of evidence as a polite suggestion. That is bad science. It is outstanding television.
